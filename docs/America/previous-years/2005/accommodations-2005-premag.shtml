89 Chestnut Hotel/Dorm/Conference Facility

The conference facility is 89 Chestnut street, which has both conference facilities and unique hotel/dorm hybrid rooms. (See their website -- http://89chestnut.com/) These are basically hotel rooms but without a TV. During the school year they are used as dorm rooms for University of Toronto students, and are used as hotel room throughout the summer months. They are only slightly more expensive than university dorm rooms and much less expensive than proper hotel rooms in downtown Toronto. This price/quality combination plus the extreme convenience of having your room in the same building as the conference leads us to believe that almost everyone will opt for this option.

For people interested in proper hotels or dorms we will try to provide other options, per the below.

Hotel

An official hotel has not been selected at this time. An announcement will be made as soon as one is chosen. owever, there are an unlimited (~ 25,000) number of hotel rooms of all prices (USD$40-USD$200/night) within easy walking distance. A "recommended" hotel nearby the conference will be identified and negotiated with for group rates closer to the date of the conference with some already in mind.

Dorms

Excellent dorm facilities are available from many different colleges within the University of Toronto. We are currently negotiating with several to find the best one for YAPC use. Any dorm location that is chosen will be of good quality, very inexpensive (approximately US$40/night/person) and very nearby the on-campus site of the conference.

Rates:

  • At 89 Chestnut, the rates for conference attendees will be approximately C$80/night. Several additional fees will apply, though... hotel taxes of approx. C$10, daily parking will be approx. C$23/day if you bring a car, optional DSL modem rental of approx. C$5/day, and a "breakfast upgrade" of approx. C$6/per breakfast will move you from the complimentary continental breakfast to a full hot breakfast. Nevertheless, this is still very cheap for downtown Toronto. And the quality of all of this is quite high.
