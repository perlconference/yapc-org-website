Food Near IIT

Posted on March 28th, 2006 by Josh

There have been quite a few questions about food choices near IIT. Most of the people that I talk with say that the selection is limited, but there are a few options that are close-by. Luckly for us, the folks at Prudential have compiled a map that details some of the restaurants around Chicago. The map doesn’t allow for deep-linking, but it’s pretty easy to find IIT. Just start at downtown Chicago and move the map down I-90/94 to the intersection of I-90/94 and 33rd Street. You should see Armor Square Park and Comiskey Park on the West side of I-90/94 and IIT on the East side of the road. The conference will be held very near to the intersection of 33rd and I-90/94. After you’ve found the location, play around with the restaurant display settings. It looks like the closest is Donnie’s Pizza and Cafe.

Updated!!!

Prudential saw that we had linked to them and sent us a deep-link into their map. Thanks Prudential!

Registration Is Open!!

Posted on March 27th, 2006 by Josh

Chicago.pm is proud to announce that registration is open for Yet Another Perl Conference North America (YAPC::NA) 2006. The primary conference will occur June 26th through 28th at the Illinois Institute of Technology in Chicago, Illinois. The conference will feature speakers from throughout the Perl community, as well as, keynote addresses from luminaries such as Larry Wall, the creator of Perl, and guest speakers from the broader dynamic languages and open source communities. The full three-day conference costs only $100, but early registrants can take advantage of a 15% discount and attend the conference for only $85. With such a low cost, YAPC is one of the most affordable and accessible technical conferences available today.

In addition to the conference, three open courses will be offered on June 29th and 30th. These courses are taught by some of the most notable Perl instructors: brian d foy, Randal Schwartz, and Damian Conway. The courses will all run simultaneously during the two days after the conference. Conference attendance is not required when signing up for classes, but it is encouraged.

Seats for the conference and for the open classes are limited, so be sure to register today and guarantee your spot at YAPC! Also, if you are planning on lodging and/or parking on-campus during the conference, or dining in the university cafeteria, you should include those items when registering.

Updated!!!
A few notes in regard to comments:

  • If you are flying to Chicago, consider booking on American Airlines at aa.com using the discount code A2566AN for a 5% discount
  • There aren’t any hotels beside IIT, so your best shot is to get a hotel downtown and take the ‘L’ to campus. At this time, we haven’t been able to negotiate any discounts with local hotels
  • As far as restaurants within walking distance of IIT, there aren’t many. I’ll try to get down there before April 1 and see what is available. In the meantime, if anyone knows, please chime in.
  • If you don’t prepay for a meal card, you can still purchase food at the cafeteria at IIT. The prepay just allows IIT the ability to properly estimate the amount of food. You can also always grab something at the snack bar in the conference center, order a pizza or some other delivery, or go up one stop on the ‘L’ to Chinatown.

Master Classes After YAPC

Posted on March 16th, 2006 by Josh

Chicago.pm, in conjunction with O’Reilly, Stongehenge, and Thoughtstream, is proud to announce post-YAPC open enrollment master classes taught by some of Perl’s most noted instructors. These classes will be held Thursday June 29th and Friday June 30th.

We currently have three courses on the roster. brian d foy will be instructing Stonehenge Consulting’s “Learning Perl” course. This course is based on the recently updated O’Reilly book, “Learning Perl”. This beginners’ course presumes no prior knowledge of Perl.

Randal Schwartz will be teaching “Intermediate Perl”, another Stonehenge course based on another recently updated O’Reilly book, “Intermediate Perl” (formerly “Learning Perl Objects, References, and Modules”). This course is geared toward programmers with a good background in the basics of Perl. The course will show you how to keep your Perl programs running smoothly, even as they grow.

Finally, Damian Conway will be conducting Thoughtstream’s “Perl Best Practices” course. This course takes Perl’s philosophy of “There’s more than one way to do it” and shows the best way to do it. The course emphasizes coding standards and best-practice techniques that are important in Perl development.

We hope that these courses will be useful to programmers of all levels of Perl experience and welcome anyone with an interest in becoming a better Perl hacker to sign up. Attendance at YAPC is not required to attend the open courses. Registration and fees are separate from the conference. You may register at the time you register for YAPC.

Chicago.pm would like to give a big thanks to brian, Randal, and Damian for agreeing to help support YAPC and The Perl Foundation. Also, we owe our deepest gratitudes to O’Reilly for offering to supply books for the courses.
