By Train

  • Amtrak

Amtrak serves Chicago at Union Station. Union station is accessible to public transportation that connects to IIT. Additionally, limousine and taxi are available at Union Station.

If you are coming by train with someone else, you may be able to use coupon code H568 on the Amtrak website to get 2 tickets for the price of 1. source

Directions from Union Station to IIT

Be sure to buy a transit card at Union Station. It's the blue machine off to the side near the Metra ticket counter, and it only accepts cash. In the past, you could pay cash on the bus, (and you still can, but if you do this, you won't get a transfer). If you don't have a transit card, you'll have to pay $2 for each bus or train you take. If you get a transit card, the second ride is $0.25, and the third is free.

Here are some possible routes:

From Union Station, take the #1 Indiana / Hyde Park bus to 33rd and Michigan; IIT is about a block away. This takes about 30 minutes.

From Union Station, take the #X28 Stony Island Express, #126 Jackson, or the #151 Sheridan, to Wabash. At Wabash, transfer the the Green Line `L', and take the Green Line south to 35th/IIT/Bronzeville. Trains are labelled "East 63rd" or "Ashland / 63rd"; trains to "Harlem / Lake" are going in the wrong direction). It takes about 5 minutes to get to Wabash, then about 8 minutes on the L to 35th.

You can also walk from Union Station, along Jackson Bvld., to the L. It's a pretty short walk; you exit Union Station, go over the river, and get on the L at Quincy / Wells. At Quincy, you'll have to take an Orange Line train to Clark / Lake, and then transfer to a southbound Green Line train, as per the above instructions.

Also, you can take the Red Line instead of the Green Line; the 35th St. station on the Red Line is a block or so away from the Green Line. Both take about the same amount of time, but the Green Line is elevated so you can see the scenery better. The Red Line is underground until 22nd St, and then in the median of an expressway until 35th. Either route gets you there, though.

Directions from O'Hare Airport to IIT

At O'Hare, follow the signs to the CTA (`L'). Get on the `L' (Blue Line), and take it downtown (either Forest Park or 54/Cermak). When you get to Washington downtown, transfer (for free) to the Red Line. Take the Red Line south (in the 95/Dan Ryan direction), and get off the Red Line at Sox/35th. From Sox/35th, walk east to IIT (i.e. away from US Cellular Field).
