There is no shortage of ways to get around town; however, one of the most economical options is to use public transportation. There are visitor passes that you can order online or can pick up once you get here at these locations. The visitor passes give you unlimited rides for fixed periods (1, 2, 3, or 5 days). Transit passes CANNOT BE PURCHASED ON CAMPUS, you will have to go to the customer service desk of the JewelOsco or purchase one in advance.

If you don't have a visitor pass, you can buy a transit card or pay by cash. With cash, every single ride on a CTA bus is $2. With the transit card it is $1.75 per bus ride, with 2 transfers within the next 2 hours for only $0.25. CTA trains always cost $2, with cash or a transit card, plus $0.25 if you need a transfer.

Be sure to buy a transit card at Union Station. It's the blue machine off to the side near the Metra ticket counter, and it only accepts cash. In the past, you could pay cash on the bus, (and you still can, but if you do this, you won't get a transfer).

Note that the CTA trains (i.e., the L) are separate from the Metra rail system, and CTA passes are not valid on them. These commuter trains reach the distant suburbs, use the same tracks as some Amtrak lines, and cost more per ride, with the cost going up the further you ride. See http://metrarail.com/riders.html. You can buy tickets from the conductors on-board the trains, but note that if the station you boarded has open ticket booths or kiosks, you will be charged an extra $2/person fee for buying from the conductor instead of the ticket booth.

The 2 nearest CTA stations to IIT are the Red line's Sox Park/35th stop (which is still a few blocks away) and the Green line's 35th/Bronzeville/IIT stop. The north platform of the latter will deposit you directly behind some of the IIT dorms. You can transfer between Red and Green lines (and the Orange line, to Midway) at the Roosevelt station.

Note that trains are labelled with the color of the line and the final destination. Thus:

  • Red line – Howard: north bound
    • from IIT, this takes you to Chinatown, the Loop, and Addison and Goose Island
  • Red line – 95th/Dan Ryan: south bound
    • From the Loop or Goose Island, this takes you to 35th/Sox Park. You can transfer to the Green line at Roosevelt to go directly back to the IIT campus stop.
  • Green line – Harlem/Lake: north to the Loop, then due west
    • transfer to Red line - Howard trains at Roosevelt to get to Addison stop & Goose Island
  • Green line – either Ashland/63rd OR East 63rd/Cottage Grove: south
    • From Roosevelt, this take you immediately back to IIT. Both of these branches stop at IIT before forking off to their final stations.
