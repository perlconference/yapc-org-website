Note: This is a plan that can be partially implemented at a grass roots level by the displaced passengers (henceforth called DPs) themselves - put those coursing glucocorticoids to good use! See "What YOU can do:" below.

The nut of my argument is that if we deal not with 150 steaming DPs but only a few groups of DPs, grouped initially by final destination and then subgrouped by one of 4 possible resolutions (excluding coronary infarct while in line) we can eliminate a large part of the emotional anguish while incurring close to the same financial costs we (speaking as the airline) would be incurring anyway, less even if people are pleased enough to not order 8 lobsters and a bottle of single malt on their comp'd hotel tab.

So what should Airline Agents (and their helper software systems) be doing as a delayed flight is approaching the point where some of its customers will miss connections?

  1. Who are the displaced persons?
  2. What are their final destinations?
  3. What is the possible set of solutions for each of the final desinations? (e.g. taxi is not an option from Dulles to Jakarta)

The possible set of solutions for a final destination would include the following queues. I tried to order them in ascending cost except for the last option.

  1. rebook: fill open seats to the destination
    1. rebook with this carrier to the same destination
    2. rebook to a nearby airport, apply this set again at that airport
    3. rebook with another carrier
  2. hotel: put up in a hotel and rebook in the morning
  3. ground transportation
  4. bug out: get the DP its baggage so it can wing it - give it some comp. miles, pilot caps, ski ball tickets, etc. as a thank you gift - these folks are out of your hair now.

Now that we know what the most probable solutions are and to what groups of folks they can be applied we can populate the solution details for a final destination:

  1. rebook: What is the time-ordered list of open seats up to the maximum count of the group, or if its a nearby destination, up to the percentage of folks who usually take this option instead of group transport.
  2. hotel: How many of those seats are late enough to require a hotel stay? What is the cost-ordered list of open airport hotel rooms to the maximum amount of this subgroup? Can we pre-book a bank of these rooms?
  3. ground transportation: What is the cost-ordered list of available airport rental cars? Are there any taxis around and from where can more be summoned if needed?
  4. bug out: What and how many tschotskes do we have to give out?

All of this data should be available to the Agents from the second the timeline slips to where flights are going to be missed. And it should be possible to define an arbitrary ordering of DPs and pre-assign them to open seats and/or rental cars and to scratch them and bump up the queue if they bug off or pick another option. I know I could do it in Perl!

What YOU can do:

Given that airlines are ridiculously hypo-staffed, you there, you with the laptop or clipboard and the extroverted personality, should be discretely - or not, you know who you are - walking the line and compiling the counts of inbound delayed flight, outbound missed/cancelled flight, and final destination tuples. You could then get this data presented to the agents or - Glory! - a passing supervisor by whatever means you luck and charisma provide you. I guess its out of your hands at that point, but at the very least you've provided the poor buggers in your line with some hope, distraction and direction.

Hey, what if every person who made it to the front of the line presented the Agent with, "There are 19 of us who need to get to Denver, what options are available to us?" and if the stoney fa Agent refuses to catch on, when the DP has gotten their personal new routing info from the Agent turn around and announce as loudly as possible to the line where they are headed and what the decision was. Again, provide some hope and direction at the very least maybe counter the airlines supposed wish to give a raw deal to some of the more passive folks in the line.
