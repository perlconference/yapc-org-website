Please add your suggestions on how to make future YAPCs even more awesome than 2006.

  • Add Beginner/Intermediate/Advanced indicator for each talk on the schedule for quick reference.
  • Try to keep the same levels of talks in the same rooms, to avoid too much moving around.
  • More power strips!
  • Post a nice big list of possible BOF locations so we don't have to run around an unfamiliar campus (or city) to find a place to hold one.
  • This was raised before. Hosting a central bullentin board somewhere so that everyone can post/update news (BOF etc). it works well for people with or without network. And it help the communication.
  • Keep presentations in relatively dark rooms – it can be hard to read the slides in the Expo (say).
  • Keep the recruiters out of the bathrooms.
  • No one should be allowed to present about inside out objects except Abigail, who probably doesn't even want to. Or for that matter, three and a half talks about any one subject is too much.
  • Keep Lightning Talk slots open for people who decide to present after seeing some other talks and becoming more comfortable with the idea – that is, keep them open until the day of the session or they're filled.
  • Encourage presenters to post urls to slides/code/outlines prior to their presentation.
  • The organizers did an outstanding job! I'd ask that the next crew post a schedule of when they'll push out certain kinds of info (parking/residence/meals/etc) so people like me won't get too anxious and bomb them with email questions
  • (From the "I Know I'm dreaming, but..." Department) There was a lot of time lost connecting pcs to the display system. It's would be nice if presenters sent their slide shows ahead so all presentation slides could be loaded and ready on a dedicated machine, ready to go.
  • Allow us to connect in to a screen of the presenter. Expo had really bad lighting, and I missed understanding much of what the presenter was talking about, as well as the front time of trying to find a font scheme where some of us could see. if we could just attach to a screen, or vnc this could be mitigated rather quickly.
  • A shorter live auction
  • More pre-auction publicity announcements, such as "for some items you will likely want to bid as a group, so get yourself together a group well before the auction so you are prepared to take advantage of it."
  • explain at the conference before hand and then at the dinner auction that the auction is a fund raiser. Have an auction demo ... sort of a skit or play ... with lively bidding wars to get people in to the spirit.
  • The overviews of the local facilities given by the hosting team was very helpful. Keep this for the future.
  • More recycle bins - there were 2, one cans, one plastic bottles, but they were behind the job fair folks at one point - dunno how many actually saw them.
  • Keep the DIY attitude - Monday AM one guy, waiting to get a parking pass, was shanghaied (press-ganged?) into making the doughnut run for the volunteers (well, he had a car, didn't he?). Another parking pass waiting geek jumped in and filled swag bags.
  • A longer live auction
  • A pre-live auction website with pics and descriptions of items to be auctioned to raise money. This will allow individuals to make arrangements for higher priced items and let them check up on things like the two training packages.
  • Spread information about how much, if any of the bids can be claimed on taxes. Not everyone is familiar with these things.
  • Possibly inform the list that "Lunch with * will be an item. It could help on two fronts. 1. A Perl Monger chapter could plan to bid as a whole ahead of time. 2. Members of a chapter may change change their mind and come for the possibility of winning the lunch.
