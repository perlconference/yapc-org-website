Chicago is the one of the finest places to dine in the world. If you crave it, we have it.

Locate a restaurant to your liking at:

  • Chicago Reader Restaurant Finder
  • Metromix Dining
  • VegChicago.com. The vegetarian and vegan guide to Chicago.
  • LTHForum is a bulletin board where the hard-core local foodies hang out and answer questions.

Walking Distance to IIT: Google Maps' list of restaurants is pretty accurate.

  • West: Taking 33rd west over the Dan Ryan you come to a small Italian neighborhood which has a few places. 31st is the boundary between it and Chinatown, which stretches to the north. A lot of the places here are small takeout spaces with limited seating. Also, a few are closed Mondays, so try to call first.
  • East on 35th there is a Church's Chicken, a Quiznos, and a few other burger/chicken/fish joints.

Near to IIT (but too far to walk):

  • Bridgeport Coffee House at Morgan & 31st is a laid-back place to chill with good joe, light eats, and free wireless.
  • Haven't tried it, but this looks interesting: Ramova's Grill
  • The legendary Chinatown, with dozens of restaurants.
  • Connie's Pizza Legendary Chicago style pizza, good ambiance.
  • WhiteCastle. Seriously, some of us miss it.
  • Review of Bridgeport Restaurants (to the West of IIT)
  • West by bus: taking the 35th bus west, past the ballpark to Halstead, are a few diners (the Ramova Grill and Bridgeport Restaurant), hot dog joints, and a good taqueria (La Mexicana). Additional Bridgeport restaurants, like Healthy Food Lithuanian and the pub Puffer's, are to the north of 35th on Halstead.

Delivery: Try the listings in GrubHub.

Shopping:

  • There is a Jewel supermarket at 35th and Martin Luther King Drive (1 mile east of IIT). It closes at 10 pm. There is also a 24-hour Walgreens here.
  • There is a 24-hour Jewel at the Green line Roosevelt station, directly to the south on Roosevelt.
  • Off of the Chinatown Red line stop are a variety of Asian groceries; see this thread

Further afield:

  • Manny's Deli. Beloved Jewish-style cafeteria deli with gigantic corned-beef sandwiches. Chicagoans from all walks of life lunch here. (Note: it closes at 4 pm.)
  • Gale Street Inn. Been described as the best ribs this side of the garden of eve. Ok, I made that up. Right across the street from the Jefferson Park Blue Line L stop. – KentCowgill
