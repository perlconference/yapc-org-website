Master Classes After YAPC

Posted on March 16th, 2006 by Josh

Chicago.pm, in conjunction with O’Reilly, Stongehenge, and Thoughtstream, is proud to announce post-YAPC open enrollment master classes taught by some of Perl’s most noted instructors. These classes will be held Thursday June 29th and Friday June 30th.

We currently have three courses on the roster. brian d foy will be instructing Stonehenge Consulting’s “Learning Perl” course. This course is based on the recently updated O’Reilly book, “Learning Perl”. This beginners’ course presumes no prior knowledge of Perl.

Randal Schwartz will be teaching “Intermediate Perl”, another Stonehenge course based on another recently updated O’Reilly book, “Intermediate Perl” (formerly “Learning Perl Objects, References, and Modules”). This course is geared toward programmers with a good background in the basics of Perl. The course will show you how to keep your Perl programs running smoothly, even as they grow.

Finally, Damian Conway will be conducting Thoughtstream’s “Perl Best Practices” course. This course takes Perl’s philosophy of “There’s more than one way to do it” and shows the best way to do it. The course emphasizes coding standards and best-practice techniques that are important in Perl development.

We hope that these courses will be useful to programmers of all levels of Perl experience and welcome anyone with an interest in becoming a better Perl hacker to sign up. Attendance at YAPC is not required to attend the open courses. Registration and fees are separate from the conference. You may register at the time you register for YAPC.

Chicago.pm would like to give a big thanks to brian, Randal, and Damian for agreeing to help support YAPC and The Perl Foundation. Also, we owe our deepest gratitudes to O’Reilly for offering to supply books for the courses.
