YAPC::NA 2006: June 26 to June 30th!

Posted on February 6th, 2006 by Pete

Chicago.pm is proud to announce that for the first time ever, YAPC::NA will be extended to five days instead of three!

June 26th through the 28th will be the “regular” conference. However, on June 29th and 30th, we will have four additional classes available at IIT. Each of these classes will be taught by a prominent figure from the Perl world.

Each presenter will be introduced over the next four weeks, along with the course they intend to teach. The courses will have an additional registration fee, and proceeds from the classes will benefit TPF.

5 months to YAPC::NA 2006!

Posted on January 26th, 2006 by Pete

Five months from today, people from all around the world will descend on Chicago for the 2006 edition of YAPC::NA. We’re putting together a YAPC that has a lot of tradition, but also breaks new ground, and we’re excited to tell you all about it - but we’re not going to do that just yet. There’s some wonderful surprises in store for the conference.

First of all, we’d like to announce a new mailing list for all things YAPC: yapc-general@perl.org. To subscribe, send a blank email to yapc-general-subscribe@perl.org and respond to the challenge email that comes back to you. We would like to encourage discussion about all things YAPC, so once you’ve subscribed, you can post to the list as well.

Second, we’ll be opening registration and providing details on accommodations very soon. We’ll be offering the same registration price as last year’s conference until near to conference time. However, after that, the cost to register will be going up. More details will be provided in the registration announcement.

Finally, I’d like to remind everyone about our Call for Participation. The deadline to submit a proposal is April 10th. We’d especially like to encourage first-time speakers to apply. We’re looking for a good variety of talks this year, including talks on converting to mod_perl2, development tools, Perl on platforms besides UNIX, and analyses of other interpreted languages (e.g. Ruby or Python).

We hope to see you in Chicago for YAPC::NA 2006, on June 26th!

Call For Participation

Posted on December 6th, 2005 by YAPC

On behalf of The Perl Foundation, Chicago.pm is officially announcing the call for participation for Yet Another Perl Conference North America 2006. The conference will be held in Chicago, Illinois on June 26th through June 28th at the Illinois Institute of Technology.

Since this is Yet Another Perl Conference, we are looking for presentations that would be of interest to Perl programmers. Typically this would be something directly related to programming Perl, but it doesn’t have to be.

In addition, this year we are branching out into the wider realm of dynamic languages. Though most all sessions will be about Perl, we are planning on slipping in a few talks on languages such as Ruby and Python. Think “Python for Perl Programmers” or “Ruby Has Rails, Why Doesn’t Perl?”

We are looking for 20, 50, and 80 minute presentations. If you aren’t quite ready to present for that long, don’t worry, a call for five minute lightning talks will be announced closer to conference time. Both individual and group presentations are welcome. Also, feel free to submit multiple proposals to increase your chances of being chosen.

Please send your submissions to papers-at-yapcchicago.org. Be sure to include a title for your presentation, an abstract, and the amount of time that you think you will need to present. If you will need some form of presentation equipment beyond a podium, microphone, and projector, please note that in your submission.

The deadline for abstract submissions is April 10th 2006, but we are more than happy to start receiving submissions today. When you submit your proposal, expect to get a confirmation within a week. If you don’t hear from us, try to resubmit.
